I am Josh Helgoe, a coral biologist,
scientific diver, and PhD candidate at FIU.

Even after severe coral bleaching events there invariably remain a few enigmatic survivors. What makes these corals so resilient?

This simple yet profoundly deep question is what galvanizes my scientific ambitions. Coral bleaching events are an ecological consequence of what is ultimately occuring physiologically to each individual coral and its symbionts. That is why I stive to understand coral resilience at the cellular level. Despite the stubborn complexity of fundamental coral biology, we must break through our current knowledge barriers to faciliate the development of potentially game-changing coral conservation strategies and technologies.
